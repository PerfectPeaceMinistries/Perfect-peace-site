Names of God

Exploring the Names and Nature of our Lord

YHWH (Yahweh) — I AM WHO I AM

God's personal name revealed to Moses. It speaks of His eternal self-existence and unchanging nature.

Adonai — Lord

A title denoting God's sovereign rule and majestic authority over all creation.

Jehovah — The LORD (Mispronunciation of YHWH)

A later vocalization of God's covenant name YHWH. Originated when scholars mistakenly combined the consonants of YHWH with the vowels of Adonai, resulting in "Jehovah." Though not the original pronunciation, it became common in early Protestant Bible translations.

El Elyon — God Most High

The supreme title for God, declaring Him as sovereign over all creation, exalted above every power and authority.

El Roi — The God Who Sees Me

The name declared by Hagar in the wilderness, revealing God's intimate care. He doesn't just see all things — He sees you. Personally. Compassionately.

El Shaddai — God Almighty / All-Sufficient One

A name revealing both God’s overwhelming power and His tender provision. He is mighty to save—and enough to sustain.

Jehovah Jireh — The LORD Will Provide

The name Abraham gave to the mountain where God provided a ram. It proclaims God’s perfect provision, ultimately fulfilled in Christ, our sacrificial Lamb.

Jehovah Rapha — The LORD Who Heals

A name that reveals God’s heart to restore and make whole—physically, spiritually, and eternally. Healing flows from His mercy and is fulfilled in Christ.
