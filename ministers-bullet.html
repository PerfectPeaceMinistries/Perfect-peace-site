The Pride Trap

Pride is subtle—and deadly. It blinds us to correction, hardens us to truth, and distances us from God. In this brief message, we expose how pride creeps into the Christian life and how the gospel calls us to humility instead.

Reaping What We Sow

Every action bears fruit. Whether good or evil, what we plant in our hearts and lives eventually grows and returns to us. This message is a sobering reminder of God's unchanging principle: we reap what we sow.

Don’t Worry

Worry accomplishes nothing—but faith moves mountains. Jesus calls us not to be anxious, but to trust in our Father who knows our needs. In this message, find peace for your heart and a renewed call to trust God completely.

Rightly Handling the Word

Scripture is not ours to shape—it is God's Word to submit to. In a time when truth is often twisted to fit preference, this message is a call to handle the Bible with reverence, accuracy, and integrity.

Negative Self-Image

You are not who the world says you are. In Christ, you are a new creation—loved, chosen, and redeemed. This message confronts the lies of shame and insecurity with the truth of your identity in Jesus.

Spiritual Blindness

Until God opens our eyes, we cannot see. Sin blinds the heart, and no one comes to the truth unless the Spirit draws them. This message exposes the reality of spiritual blindness—and the miracle of divine awakening.

Only God Is Good

We are not good people who need a little help—we are sinners in need of a Savior. Jesus said, “No one is good except God alone.” This message calls us to lay aside self-righteousness and recognize our desperate need for grace.

True Faith Translates into Obedience

Faith without obedience is not saving faith. True belief in Christ leads to submission, repentance, and a changed life. This message calls us to examine ourselves—not just by what we say we believe, but by how we live.

Salvation Is Through Faith

We are saved by grace alone, through faith alone, in Christ alone. No amount of effort, morality, or religious activity can earn salvation. This message lifts high the sufficiency of Christ and the simplicity of trusting in Him.

Rejoice in Our Sufferings

Suffering is not meaningless in the life of a believer. Through trials, God produces endurance, character, and hope. This message reminds us that even in pain, we are being shaped into the image of Christ—and that is cause for rejoicing.

His Power Made Perfect in Weakness

God does not need our strength—He displays His power through our weakness. When we are at our lowest, His grace is sufficient. This message encourages us to stop hiding our frailty and instead boast in it, for there His glory is revealed.

Original Sin

We are not born innocent—we are born in sin. Original sin means every part of our being is affected by the fall. This message explains the weight of our depravity and the glorious grace of the God who saves sinners.

Dead to Sin

If we are in Christ, sin no longer reigns over us. We have died with Him and been raised to walk in newness of life. This message proclaims our freedom—not to sin, but from sin—by the power of the risen Christ.

Sin That Dwells in Us

Even after we are made new, the flesh still lingers. The Christian life is marked by war — the Spirit within us fighting against the sin that remains. This message acknowledges the struggle, but reminds us that Christ is greater, and His grace is sufficient for the battle.

No Condemnation

There is no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus. This message declares the freedom we have in Him—free from guilt, free from shame, and free from the wrath of God. Christ bore our judgment so we could walk in peace and assurance.
